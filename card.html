Scorpion

Scorpions number about 1750 described species,[3] with 13 extant families recognised to date. Only about 25 of these species are known to have venom capable of killing a human being

  • Scientific name: Scorpiones
  • Class: Arachnida
  • Order: Scorpiones; C. L. Koch, 1837
  • Phylum: Arthropoda

Scorpions are predatory arachnids of the order Scorpiones. They have eight legs[1] and are easily recognized by the pair of grasping pedipalps and the narrow, segmented tail, often carried in a characteristic forward curve over the back, ending with a venomous stinger.
